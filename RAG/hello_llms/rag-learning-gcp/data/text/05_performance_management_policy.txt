# Performance Management Policy

## Purpose
This policy outlines the framework for setting expectations, evaluating performance, and supporting employee development.

## Goal Setting
- Employees and managers will set SMART goals at the beginning of each performance cycle (January).
- Goals must align with team and company objectives.
- Goals may be revised mid-year with mutual agreement.

## Performance Reviews
- Formal performance reviews are conducted twice a year: mid-year (June) and year-end (December).
- Reviews assess achievement of goals, competencies, and overall contribution.
- Employees complete a self-assessment prior to each review.

## Performance Ratings
- Exceeds Expectations: Consistently surpasses goals and demonstrates exceptional performance.
- Meets Expectations: Achieves goals and meets all role requirements.
- Needs Improvement: Partially meets goals; a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) will be initiated.
- Unsatisfactory: Fails to meet minimum requirements; may result in termination.

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- A PIP outlines specific improvement targets and a timeline (typically 60-90 days).
- Regular check-ins will be scheduled during the PIP period.
- Failure to meet PIP targets may result in termination.

## Recognition
- Outstanding performers are eligible for bonuses, promotions, and public recognition.
- Peer nominations for recognition can be submitted quarterly to HR.
