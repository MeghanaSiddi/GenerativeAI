# Disciplinary and Grievance Policy

## Purpose
To provide a fair and consistent process for addressing employee misconduct and resolving workplace grievances.

## Disciplinary Process

### Minor Misconduct
- Step 1: Verbal warning issued by the manager, documented in the employee file.
- Step 2: Written warning if the behavior continues within 6 months.
- Step 3: Final written warning with a clear improvement timeline.

### Serious Misconduct
Serious misconduct may result in immediate suspension or termination without prior warnings. Examples include:
- Theft or fraud
- Physical violence or threats
- Gross negligence
- Serious breach of confidentiality

### Disciplinary Hearing
- Employees will be given at least 48 hours notice before a disciplinary hearing.
- Employees have the right to be accompanied by a colleague or union representative.
- A written outcome will be provided within 5 business days of the hearing.

## Grievance Process
- Employees with a workplace grievance should first attempt to resolve it informally with their manager.
- If unresolved, a formal grievance can be submitted in writing to HR.
- HR will acknowledge the grievance within 3 business days and schedule a hearing within 10 business days.
- Employees will receive a written decision within 5 business days of the hearing.

## Appeals
- Employees may appeal any disciplinary or grievance decision within 10 business days of receiving the outcome.
- Appeals are reviewed by a senior manager not involved in the original decision.
